About

Yniversalist is a tool for converting between Cyrillic and Latin alphabets.

Transliteration is the process of converting text from one writing system to another, focusing on preserving the written appearance by representing characters of one alphabet with corresponding characters of another (e.g. Ukrainian –®–µ–≤—á–µ–Ω–∫–æ to Latin ≈†evƒçenko). Transcription, on the other hand, focuses on preserving pronunciation rather than spelling by representing the sounds of speech in a standardised way (e.g. Ukrainian –®–µ–≤—á–µ–Ω–∫–æ to English Shevchenko).

Bidirectional Conversion

Yniversalist supports bidirectional conversion between Cyrillic and Latin scripts. Language-Style combinations that reliably support this two-way conversion are marked with ‚Üª in the documentation.

Favourites

You can save up to ten frequently used language-style combinations as favourites. Favourites can be deleted by clicking on the star ‚òÜ next to the language of the respective favourite.

Privacy Notice

Yniversalist does not collect or store any of your data. Your data, including favourised combinations, conversion history, and theme settings, are stored locally in your browser cache. You can remove this data by clearing your browser cache. For information on data collection by GitHub Pages, which hosts Yniversalist, please refer to their privacy statement.

Note on Character Normalisation

+

When reverse converting from Latin to Cyrillic, it might happen that the input text contains unicode characters not represented in the conversion mappings used by Yniversalist. This concerns primarily the use of combined characters, whereas the character mappings predominantly consist of precomposed characters, but also potential OCR errors resulting in the inclusion of similar looking characters. To reduce the number of errors, these characters (both uppercase and lowercase) are automatically exchanged with characters represented in the conversion mapping to ensure proper conversion.

If characters in your input match the normalisation mapping, you'll receive a notification about any replacements, provided notifications are permitted.

Romanian
Overview

Yniversalist supports three varieties of Romanian Cyrillic: the Bessarabian/Moldovan variety (which uses a variant of the Russian Civil Script), and two historical varieties used in Wallachia and the Old Kingdom until approximately 1880 (Slavonic and Transitional).

The Slavonic and Transitional scripts can be converted to approximated Latin Romanian text that is more legible and suitable for machine translation. These scripts can also be transliterated according to the ALA-LC Romanian Cyrillic transliteration standard.

Additionally, the Moldovan standard can be converted to other Russian-based scholarly standards to comply with library cataloging practices.
